The Plant Is The Problem

Kratom leaf naturally contains the same alkaloids as extracts, shots, and 7‑OH products. There is no “safe” version. All forms are unregulated opioids.

One Plant, One Danger

Some lawmakers have been told that “natural” kratom leaf is safer than extracts or “synthetic” 7‑OH products. That is false. The kratom plant naturally contains two primary psychoactive alkaloids: mitragynine and 7‑hydroxymitragynine (7‑OH). These are the exact same compounds that manufacturers concentrate to make extracts and shots. There is no chemical difference between the mitragynine in a dried leaf and the mitragynine in a vape shop extract – only the concentration.

Key fact: The human body also converts mitragynine into 7‑OH after ingestion. Even “plain leaf” produces the potent opioid‑like effects that drive addiction, overdose, and death.

When industry lobbyists say “we only oppose synthetic 7‑OH,” they are using a distraction. Natural leaf already contains 7‑OH, and the body makes more of it from mitragynine. A “natural‑only” bill would leave the exact same dangerous products on gas station shelves – just without the word “synthetic” on the label.

The Same Alkaloids, Different Forms

Raw Leaf / Powder

Contains natural mitragynine and 7‑OH. The original plant material. Already an opioid.

Mitragynine (MGM) Extracts

Concentrated mitragynine from the same plant. Higher potency, same pharmacology.

7‑OH Products

Highly concentrated 7‑hydroxymitragynine – already present in natural leaf. Just a more potent version of the same drug.

Whether it’s a capsule of ground leaf, a tincture, or a “7‑OH” tablet, the active ingredients are the same. The only differences are potency and speed of absorption. Regulating one form while allowing another is like saying “only powdered cocaine is illegal, but crack is fine.” It makes no sense.

The “Synthetic‑Only” Trap

Some state bills attempt to ban only “synthetic” kratom alkaloids while leaving “natural” leaf legal. This is a deliberate loophole. Because:

  • There is no field test to distinguish synthetic from natural mitragynine or 7‑OH.
  • Manufacturers can simply call their extract “natural” and continue selling.
  • The natural leaf already contains the same addictive opioid‑like compounds.

The only honest policy is a full ban on all kratom products – natural leaf, extracts, shots, and synthetic derivatives. Any bill that leaves “natural” kratom legal is a gift to the industry, not a protection for the public.

What we demand: Schedule I classification for all kratom alkaloids, regardless of origin. No “natural” exemptions. No 2% caps that are impossible to enforce. A complete prohibition.
