CONTACT YOUR STATE HEALTH DEPARTMENT

They enforce food and drug safety laws. They have the power to stop the sale of adulterated products. The industry's "KCPA" doesn't change that. Make them do their job.

Why Health Departments? Find Yours

Why Your State Health Department Has the Power

Every state has its own Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act – laws that mirror the federal standard. These laws define a product as adulterated if it contains a poisonous or deleterious substance that may render it injurious to health. The FDA has already determined that kratom products meet this definition.

Key point: A state "Kratom Consumer Protection Act" (KCPA) does not make an adulterated product unadulterated. Industry-written KCPAs create labeling rules and age limits, but they do not override the state's existing food safety laws. Kratom remains adulterated – and therefore illegal to sell – regardless of what a KCPA says.

⛔ The Industry's Dirty Secret

They push KCPAs to create the illusion of legality. But your health department's adulteration laws are still on the books – and they are being ignored. Demand enforcement.

What to Demand From Your Health Department

  • Enforce state adulteration laws. Remind them that under state FD&C acts, kratom is an adulterated food/drug. Demand they issue cease-and-desist letters to retailers, seize products, and levy fines.
  • Issue a public health advisory. Ask them to warn consumers that kratom products are adulterated, unapproved, and linked to serious harm – regardless of any KCPA.
  • Investigate complaints. If you know a specific store selling kratom, file a formal complaint. Demand an investigation and enforcement action.
  • Reject industry claims that KCPA legalizes kratom. Ask for a written opinion: Does the KCPA override the state's adulteration provisions? The answer is no – but they need to say it publicly.
  • Support legislation to explicitly ban sales. Ask the health department to testify in favor of a true ban, not industry-written "regulation."

I ask your department to: - Issue a public advisory stating that kratom products remain adulterated under state law. - Investigate retailers selling kratom and enforce our adulteration laws. - Provide a written opinion clarifying that the KCPA does not legalize the sale of an adulterated substance. The industry hopes you will ignore your own laws. Please do not let them succeed. Thank you for protecting the health of our communities. [Your Name] [Your Phone Number] [Your Email]

⛔ Tips for Maximum Impact

  • Know your state law. Search for "[State] Food Drug and Cosmetic Act adulteration definition." Quote it in your message.
  • Cite the FDA. Mention that the FDA has already declared kratom adulterated – this gives your state health department federal backing.
  • Reference New York's contradiction. In New York, the KCPA passed while older adulteration law remains on the books – creating legal chaos. Use that as a warning: "Don't let our state have the same contradiction."
  • File a formal complaint. Most health departments have online forms for reporting unsafe products. File one for every retailer you know.
  • Ask for a response. Request that they clarify in writing how they interpret the interaction between the KCPA and the adulteration statute.
