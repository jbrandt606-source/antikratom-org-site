FDA Position on Kratom

Summary of the U.S. Food and Drug Administration's official statements, warnings, and regulatory position regarding kratom products.

View Summary Open FDA Document
FDA Overview

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration states that there are currently no FDA-approved drug products containing kratom or its primary alkaloids.

The agency has warned consumers not to use kratom for medical treatment because its safety and effectiveness have not been established through the FDA drug approval process.

Key FDA Findings
  • Kratom products are widely sold online and in retail stores despite lacking FDA approval.
  • No kratom drug products have been proven safe and effective for any medical condition.
  • FDA warns of risks including liver toxicity, seizures, substance use disorder, and withdrawal.
  • Kratom has been linked to neonatal withdrawal in infants exposed during pregnancy.
  • Contamination with Salmonella and heavy metals has also been documented in kratom products.
Regulatory Status

FDA states that kratom is not lawfully marketed in the United States as a drug, dietary supplement, or food additive.

The agency has taken enforcement actions including warning letters, product seizures, and collaboration with federal partners to limit illegal kratom products entering U.S. commerce.
