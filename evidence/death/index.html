Overview

Mortality surveillance systems distinguish between substances that are detected during toxicology testing and substances determined by medical examiners to be involved in the cause of death.

Detection alone does not establish causation, but it provides important context for understanding patterns of substance use in overdose investigations.

SUDORS: Kratom/Mitragynine in Overdose Deaths (2020–2024)

The State Unintentional Drug Overdose Reporting System (SUDORS) compiles detailed information on unintentional and undetermined-intent overdose deaths from participating jurisdictions. Abstractors collect data from death certificates, medical examiner/coroner reports, and post-mortem toxicology. SUDORS is designed for signal detection and prevention planning; it does not by itself establish causation or national incidence.

CDC SUDORS Mortality Analysis (2016–2017)

CDC analyzed overdose deaths recorded in the State Unintentional Drug Overdose Reporting System (SUDORS).

  • 152 deaths had mitragynine detected on toxicology
  • 91 deaths were classified as kratom-involved
  • Most deaths involved multiple substances
  • Fentanyl was the most common co-occurring substance

CDC Poison Center Surveillance (2010–2015)

A CDC analysis of National Poison Data System reports examined kratom exposures reported to poison centers across the United States.

  • 660 kratom exposure calls reported
  • 10-fold increase between 2010 and 2015
  • Common symptoms included tachycardia, agitation, nausea and hypertension
