Overview

The studies summarized here include controlled clinical trials and pharmacokinetic investigations examining how kratom and its alkaloids behave in the human body.

Most controlled studies evaluate short-term administration in healthy volunteers and measure plasma concentrations, subjective effects, and adverse events.

Huestis Safety & Tolerability Trial (2026)

Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial evaluating oral kratom (MitraLeaf) in healthy adults.

  • 116 participants
  • Four dose levels (6.65–53.2 mg mitragynine)
  • Single dose followed by 15-day repeated dosing phase
  • 23-day follow-up

Most common adverse events included nausea, dizziness, headache, somnolence, and mild increases in liver enzymes.

Human Pharmacokinetics of Mitragynine (2024)

Controlled pharmacokinetic study measuring blood concentrations of mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine after oral kratom dosing.

  • 7-OH detected primarily from metabolic conversion
  • Mitragynine steady state ~8-9 days
  • Long terminal half-life (~40–60 hours)

FDA-Registered Clinical Study

ClinicalTrials.gov study NCT06072170 evaluated kratom under controlled clinical conditions.

  • Healthy adult participants
  • Measured plasma mitragynine concentrations
  • Subjective effects including drug-liking scores
  • Adverse event monitoring

Summary Across Controlled Studies
  • 7-hydroxymitragynine appears largely through metabolism of mitragynine.
  • Exposure increases with dose and repeated administration.
  • Short-term side effects include nausea, dizziness, and sedation.
  • Controlled trials do not evaluate long-term daily use or retail product variability.
