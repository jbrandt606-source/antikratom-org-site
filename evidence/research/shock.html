KCPA Laws Are a Deadly Failure.
Only Full Bans Work.

New peer‑reviewed study of 8,919 kratom poison center exposures: “Consumer protection” acts do nothing to reduce severe outcomes, hospitalizations, or deaths. Banning kratom cuts poisonings by more than half.

🏛️ The Study That Exposes the Industry’s Favorite Lie

For years, the kratom lobby has pushed “Kratom Consumer Protection Acts” (KCPA) as the responsible middle ground – age limits, labels, and purity standards. They claimed regulation would make kratom safer. They were lying.

A new nationwide study published in Addiction (Comstock et al., 2026) analyzed every kratom exposure reported to U.S. poison centers over 14 years – 8,919 cases. The conclusion is brutal and unambiguous:

🔥 KCPA laws had no effect. States with KCPA laws had the same rates of poisonings, hospitalizations, and severe medical outcomes as states with zero regulation. Only full state bans actually protected the public.

The study covers 2010‑2023, the period when kratom went from obscure herb to gas‑station opioid. Cases exploded from 19 in 2010 to 1,242 in 2023 – a 65‑fold increase. Severe outcomes (major effects or death) rose from zero to 158 in the same period.

When researchers compared banned states to all other regulatory categories combined, the difference was stark: 2.5 times more exposures, 3.2 times more severe outcomes, and 2.4 times more hospitalizations in non‑ban states. All differences were statistically significant (p < 0.001).

Even more damning: there was no statistically significant difference between KCPA states and states with no regulation at all. The industry’s signature policy is a complete failure.

💀 What “Severe Outcomes” Actually Look Like

The study tracked cases coded as “major effect” (life‑threatening or resulting in significant residual disability) or death. Across all exposures:

  • 13% of all kratom exposures resulted in a severe medical outcome.
  • 34% required hospitalization.
  • 15% were admitted to critical care units (ICU).
  • 174 deaths were reported, including 27 single‑substance kratom fatalities where no other drugs were involved.
🧠 Single‑substance deaths: Five of six cases determined to be “probably” or “undoubtedly responsible” were males under 30 found in cardiac arrest after kratom use. No clear warning symptoms – just sudden collapse. This is not a supplement. This is a lethal drug.

Even children were not spared. Poison centers received 490 reports of kratom exposure in children under 12, including a 2‑day‑old infant with tremors and cardiac instability. The bill of “responsible adult use” doesn’t cover the infants poisoned by careless parents.

🧪 Why KCPA Laws Can’t Work – And Bans Do

The researchers offer a simple explanation: KCPA laws only add paper requirements (labels, age verification, purity claims). But kratom is still on every gas station shelf. Availability drives exposure. Bans remove the product entirely.

As the authors note: “Regulatory approaches short of an outright ban were not associated with lower rates of exposure, healthcare utilization or severe outcomes when compared to unrestricted states.” In other words, regulation is theater. It creates the illusion of safety while leaving the poison perfectly accessible.

The study also highlights the fatal enforcement problem: there is no field test to distinguish “compliant” kratom from contaminated or high‑potency extracts. Retailers ignore age limits. Labels are self‑reported. KCPA laws are unenforceable by design – exactly how the industry wants them.
