“Healthcare failed me” — The silent verdict on 2.6 million professionals

When a witness stands before a state Senate committee and says, “Kratom saved my life because nothing else worked,” legislators rarely challenge the implication. But the implication is devastating: the American healthcare system — with its doctors, nurses, psychologists, pain specialists and addiction medicine — is functionally useless. That is the hidden insult woven into every pro-kratom sob story that lawmakers use to block regulation.

If a lawmaker truly believes that a vape shop powder outperforms decades of medical research, they are voting “no confidence” in every physician who took the Hippocratic oath. Either the system works — or it doesn’t. And if the only answer is a gas station botanical, then the legislator is admitting they’ve allowed healthcare to rot.

The ridiculous double standard: wellness claims vs. medical records

Kratom advocates demand grieving families produce autopsies, toxicology reports, and death certificates. They demand forensic rigor when the outcome is harm. But when someone claims kratom “cures depression” or “manages opioid withdrawal,” they bring a fuzzy selfie and a blurry screenshot taken at 2:35AM (see above). And lawmakers accept it. No physician notes. No treatment history. Just IMG_6880.jpeg and a compelling story.

That’s not evidence. That’s an insult to every clinician who spent a decade in training.

You cannot have it both ways

Lawmakers cannot stand on a podium and boast about their state’s “world-class healthcare infrastructure” while simultaneously arguing that desperate citizens must rely on head shops because medicine has catastrophically failed them. Either healthcare works, or it doesn’t. If your justification for kratom is “patients have nowhere else to turn,” then you are confessing that your healthcare system is a hollowed-out disaster.

  • To Physicians: “Your residency, board exams, and clinical judgment are worth less than a bag of powder next to nicotine vapes.”
  • To Addiction Specialists: “We trust a convenience store employee more than your MAT protocols.”
  • To Pharmacists: “Skip the rigorous quality control — unregulated plant matter is fine.”
  • To Pain Management Experts: “Your multimodal plans are obsolete; just buy unlabeled kratom capsules.”

If the system is broken, fix it. Expand mental health access. Fund addiction medicine. Rebuild pain management. But don’t insult healthcare professionals by pretending the answer is an unregulated botanical sold at gas stations — while propping up “evidence” like a 260KB screenshot saved in a cluttered photo roll.

From the gas station to the hearing room: the wellness fantasy

“Pure natural kratom as a wellness product.” That’s the slogan. Yet the “wellness” industry has standards: third-party testing, adverse event reporting, licensed manufacturing. Kratom has none. Its advocates rely on emotional testimony and blurry IMG_8669.jpeg shots. Meanwhile, healthcare providers follow evidence-based guidelines, submit to peer review, and accept malpractice liability. The audacity of telling a nurse practitioner that “kratom worked when you couldn’t” — without a single medical record — is breathtaking. But legislators let it slide, because the insult is invisible.

No physician should accept this narrative. No pharmacist should stay silent. Every time a lawmaker invokes the “kratom success story,” they should be forced to answer: “Where are the medical records showing supervised treatment failure and subsequent kratom remission?” Without them, the story is just a story — and the insult to healthcare stands.

Direct insult: Dear healthcare provider

Lawmakers who defend kratom based on “nothing else worked” are publicly calling you incompetent. They are saying your prescription pad, your stethoscope, your referral to a pain clinic — none of it matters compared to a $20 bag from a smoke shop.

And to justify that position, they point to images like the ones above: random camera roll exports, timestamped at 2:35AM, with no clinical context.

  • No ICD-10 diagnosis codes
  • No physician progress notes
  • No objective treatment response data
  • No adverse effect documentation
  • Zero licensed medical oversight

Yet lawmakers accept this as proof that you failed your patients. Are you willing to let that stand?

The Anti-Kratom Association calls on every medical board, nursing association, and physician coalition to denounce this insult. Kratom is not medicine. And a handful of unsorted phone images are not evidence of healthcare failure.

Stop the insult. Fix healthcare, not the gas station.

Lawmakers: if patients truly cannot access effective treatment, expand Medicaid, fund addiction services, deploy multidisciplinary pain clinics. But do not stand before doctors and tell them a vape shop product outperforms their life’s work — especially when the “proof” looks like a chaotic photo album at 2:35AM.
