The “Proof” That Medicine Has Failed

This is what legislators accept as justification for keeping kratom legal. Social media ads, a “secret ingredient” wink, and a pink hippopotamus. No diagnostic codes, no treatment plans, no physician signatures.

“With a secret ingredient” — no clinical data, no physician oversight
Kratom shots — unregulated potency, zero safety studies
“READY TO LEVEL UP?” — marketing addiction as wellness
A pink hippopotamus — the standard of “medical evidence”

“Healthcare failed me” is a vote of no confidence

For decades, physicians, pharmacists, nurses, psychologists, addiction specialists, physical therapists, and pain specialists have devoted their careers to treating these conditions. Yet every time a lawmaker repeats the claim that kratom is necessary because “it helps people when nothing else works,” they are implicitly telling healthcare professionals that their work is inadequate. They are saying that a bag of unregulated powder sold next to energy drinks accomplishes what modern medicine cannot.

You cannot have it both ways

Lawmakers cannot praise their state’s healthcare infrastructure while simultaneously arguing that people must rely on products sold at convenience stores because medicine has failed them. Either healthcare works, or it does not. And if the argument for kratom is that patients have nowhere else to turn, then lawmakers should stop defending kratom and start asking why they have allowed a system to exist in which a gas station is viewed as a healthcare destination.

  • To physicians: Your residency, board exams, and clinical judgment are worth less than a bag of powder next to nicotine vapes.
  • To addiction specialists: We trust a convenience store employee more than your medication assisted treatment protocols.
  • To pharmacists: Skip rigorous quality control; unregulated plant matter is fine.
  • To pain management experts: Your multimodal plans are obsolete; just buy unlabeled kratom capsules.

The double standard: autopsies for harm, selfies for benefit

Kratom trade advocates routinely obtain autopsy reports, toxicology reports, death certificates, and medical examiner records. They demand grieving families prove every detail. But when a witness claims kratom treated their chronic pain or opioid use disorder, the “evidence” is often a social media ad saved to a camera roll at 2:35 AM. No physician notes. No treatment history. Just a pink hippopotamus and an emotional testimony.

If lawmakers truly believe these stories justify keeping kratom on store shelves, then they should apply the same standard: every pro kratom witness should be required to submit medical documentation showing the diagnosis, prior treatment failures, and a licensed healthcare provider’s note that kratom was the solution.

The solution is not a vape shop. It is fixing healthcare.

If patients cannot access effective treatment for pain, addiction, anxiety, or depression, expand treatment. Fund addiction care. Fix pain management. Increase mental health services. But do not tell healthcare professionals that the answer is a product sold in vape shops and gas stations. That is a vote of no confidence in the healthcare system.

Direct message to lawmakers

You are being asked to believe that a product promoted alongside “secret ingredient” posts and a plush pink hippopotamus outperforms surgeons, psychiatrists, and board certified pain doctors. That is an insult to every healthcare professional in your state.

  • Show the medical records, not the social media ads.
  • Show the physician’s diagnosis and treatment failure.
  • Show the objective improvement under medical supervision.
  • Stop accepting social media ads as clinical evidence.

If grieving families are expected to produce autopsies and toxicology reports, then pro kratom witnesses should submit medical documentation to the legislators they are trying to influence. One standard. Not two.

The Anti-Kratom Association calls on medical boards to reject this insult. Demand that every pro kratom witness submit their full medical records, or stop pretending that healthcare has failed.

Stop the insult. Invest in real healthcare.

Lawmakers: expand treatment. Fund addiction medicine. Strengthen pain management. Do not tell physicians they are incompetent while accepting social media ads as proof. Demand medical records, not camera roll screenshots taken at 2:35 AM.
