Identical Copy-Pasted Comments

The most obvious evidence of astroturfing is the repeated appearance of identical text across dozens of comments:

  • Example 1 (repeated 200+ times): "Say no to fake kratom reefer madness. We do not want 1980s boomer war on drugs and arresting people for a plant. We want safe accuse to natural kratom. We see this for what it is a money grab by Corrupt bureaucrats"
  • Example 2 (repeated 200+ times): "G-Protein Biased Partial Agonism proves that kratom is not just 'different' than pharmaceutical opioids it is biologically safer at the cellular level. The G-Protein vs. β-Arrestin Signaling Pathway... Lane A: The G-Protein Pathway (Analgesia)... Lane B: The β-Arrestin Pathway (Side Effects)"

These are not spontaneous individual opinions. They are form letters generated by advocacy campaigns.

The Worthington, Ohio Cluster

Hundreds of comments originated from Worthington, Ohio – the home of Jack Smith, CEO of Life of Kratom, a chain of kratom retail stores. Smith was quoted stating his advocacy strategy:

  • "We have a list of everything for people to contact. The Statehouse, DeWine's office, the Board of Pharmacy, and just kind of just keep being a pain and, like, show them, 'Hey, we're not going away.'"

These comments share identical talking points, language, and conspiracy narratives.

Fabricated Identities

One comment was submitted under the name "Colonel Charles A. Jones" claiming to be the Superintendent of the Ohio State Highway Patrol. This is a fabricated identity. The actual Colonel of the OSHP would not submit a public comment in this manner.

  • "I have proudly served as a police officer in District 3 for 25 years... I strongly urge you to stand by evidence-based approaches and allow Ohioans to continue to have access to safe, regulated kratom."

This is an attempt to create false law enforcement authority for an advocacy position.
