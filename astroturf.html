Methodology

This analysis examines all 2,370 individual comments submitted to the Ohio Board of Pharmacy regarding the proposed rule to classify mitragynine (kratom) as a Schedule I controlled substance. The full record was obtained through public records request.

Data Collection

  • All comments were extracted from the official rulemaking record provided by the Ohio Board of Pharmacy.
  • The record includes spreadsheet style tables with commenter location and stated position, long form text submissions, and attachments.

Classification Criteria

Each comment was classified using a multi step methodology:

  • Duplicate Identification: Comments were compared using text similarity analysis. Any comment containing identical or near identical language blocks of 50+ words was flagged as a duplicate.
  • Advocacy Generated vs. Genuine: Comments were classified as advocacy generated if they met any of the following criteria: duplicate text appearing in 5+ submissions, form letter structure with fill in the blank fields, technical copy paste language appearing verbatim in multiple submissions from non experts, fabricated identity with false professional credentials, or coordinated talking points shared with 10+ other comments.
  • Genuine Classification: Comments were classified as genuine if they contained unique personal narrative details, referenced specific personal experiences such as loss of a family member or personal health history, did not match any form letter template, and did not contain identical language blocks from other submissions.

Limitations

This methodology likely underestimates the true extent of advocacy generated comments. Some form letters may have been personalized by individual submitters, making them harder to detect through automated text matching. Additionally, some genuine commenters may have incorporated advocacy talking points into otherwise personal testimony, blurring the line between organic and manufactured opposition.
