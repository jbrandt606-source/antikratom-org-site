Contact Your Legislator

Your voice matters. Lawmakers need to hear from constituents about the dangers of unregulated kratom sales. Use the tools below to find and contact your representatives at both the state and federal level.

Find Your Legislators Tips for Contacting
Why Your Voice Matters

Legislators at both the state and federal level are actively considering bills that would either restrict kratom sales or, conversely, legalize and regulate it through industry-backed “Kratom Consumer Protection Acts.” Your representatives need to hear the real-world consequences of kratom - the poison control calls, the family stories, the mortality data.

A single phone call, email, or in‑district meeting can be the difference between a legislator voting to protect public health or voting to expand the market.

Step 2: Choose Your Contact Method

Legislators offer multiple ways to be reached. Each has its own advantages.

📞 Phone Call

Best for: Quick, urgent messages.

You'll speak with a staffer. Be polite, brief, and state your position clearly. Ask for your position to be noted.

📧 Email / Web Form

Best for: Detailed, thoughtful messages.

Write a concise letter. Include your full address so they know you're a constituent. Attach relevant evidence if appropriate.

✉️ Postal Mail

Best for: Formal correspondence.

Use district office addresses during recess, capitol addresses when in session [citation:3]. Allow extra delivery time.

🤝 In‑Person Meeting

Best for: Building a relationship.

Call the district office to schedule an appointment. Prepare a one‑page leave‑behind with your key points.

Step 3: Tips for Effective Contact
  • Identify yourself as a constituent. Open with your name and address.
  • Be clear and concise. State the bill number (if known) and whether you support or oppose it.
  • Share your personal story. Why does a kratom ban (scheduling) matter to you? Personal stories are powerful.
  • Stick to one issue per contact. Don't dilute your message.
  • Be respectful. Even if you disagree, politeness is remembered.
  • Ask for a response. “Will you support banning kratom products?”
  • Follow up. A thank‑you note after a meeting or call reinforces your message.

Sample Message

“My name is [Your Name], and I live at [Your Address] in your district. I am writing to urge you to support legislation that restricts the sale of kratom products. As a constituent, I am concerned about the rising number of poison control calls and the lack of FDA oversight. Please vote YES on [Bill Number] to protect our community. Thank you for your service.”

Go Deeper: Track Bills & Testify

Beyond contacting your own legislators, you can engage directly with the legislative process:

  • Track legislation: Use your state legislature's website (like ILGA.gov in Illinois) to monitor kratom-related bills.
  • File a witness slip: In many states, you can submit formal testimony (a “witness slip”) online for or against a bill – even without attending a hearing.
  • Attend committee hearings: Most state legislatures stream hearings online. You can also attend in person to show public interest.
