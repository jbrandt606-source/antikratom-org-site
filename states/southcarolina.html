South Carolina: HB 4641 is a Sham

It bans "synthetic" kratom – which doesn't exist – while leaving dangerous natural leaf completely legal. No testing. No enforcement. Just a fake ban.

DESTROY THIS BILL → CONTACT SENATORS

📜 The "Synthetic" Lie (Read the Bill)

🔥 There is no such thing as "synthetic kratom" sold in gas stations. Every product on the shelf comes from the dried leaves of the mitragyna speciosa plant. The bill's distinction is invented – a prop to claim a ban while changing nothing.

What HB 4641 actually does – and doesn't do:

  • Synthetic kratom doesn't exist. The bill targets a ghost. Real kratom – the opioid sold at every vape shop – is untouched.
  • Even if synthetic existed, there's no testing. No lab required. No random inspections. No way to distinguish "natural" from "synthetic" without expensive mass spectrometry that no one is doing.
  • No enforcement agency. Who checks? DHEC? SLED? The bill names no one, funds nothing, creates no penalties.
  • No pre‑market safety review. Products can be sold before any lead, ethanol, or bacteria test. The "USP solvent standards" are a joke without enforcement.
  • No age verification that works. Online sales continue. Gas station clerks don't card.

This bill is not a ban. It is a legislative cover story. It lets lawmakers say "we did something" while the exact same dangerous products stay on every shelf.

📄 READ THE BILL – SEE THE LOOPHOLE

⚠️ "Natural" Kratom is Already an Opioid

The bill's defenders will say: "We only banned synthetic. Natural is fine." That is a lie. Natural kratom contains mitragynine – a partial mu‑opioid agonist with proven addiction liability, withdrawal syndrome, and documented overdose deaths.

🧪 Contamination is real, and HB 4641 does nothing about it:
• Lead: 0.6–1.0 µg/g in popular powders. Daily use = chronic lead ingestion.
• Ethanol: 15‑16% in liquid extracts – legally alcoholic. Kansas seized identical products.
• No testing required. No inspector. No fine. Just self‑certification.
• No warning label. Pregnant women, people in recovery, teens – none are warned.

The bill does not require a single lab test before sale. It references "USP <467> residual solvent standards" but provides no mechanism to verify compliance. That is not regulation. That is a press release.

🎯 What to Demand (Use These Exact Points)

When you email or call, focus on these failures. Don't let them hide behind "we banned synthetic."

  • The "synthetic" distinction is a fraud. No such product is sold. The bill bans a fantasy while protecting the real danger.
  • Even if synthetic existed, you have no test. Laboratories are not funded. Inspectors are not hired. The law is unenforceable.
  • Natural kratom is an opioid. It causes addiction, withdrawal, and has been found in hundreds of death investigations.
  • Lead and ethanol are documented. HB 4641 does nothing to stop contaminated products from reaching consumers.
  • There is no enforcement. No agency, no budget, no penalties. The bill is theater.
📢 One demand: "Strike the word 'synthetic' from Section 2. Ban ALL kratom alkaloids, natural or otherwise. Fund mandatory pre‑market testing for heavy metals and ethanol. Assign enforcement with real penalties."
