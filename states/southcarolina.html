South Carolina: HB 4641 is a Fraud

It bans synthetic kratom but leaves natural leaf – an unregulated opioid – completely legal. No testing for lead. No testing for alcohol. No enforcement. Just a pretend ban.

DESTROY THIS BILL → CONTACT SENATORS

📜 What HB 4641 Actually Does (Read the Fine Print)

🔥 The Loophole Is Enormous: Section 2 adds to Schedule I only "synthetically derived compound of the plant mitragyna speciosa". Natural kratom leaf – the product sold in every vape shop and gas station – remains 100% legal. The bill explicitly exempts the plant itself.

Here's what the bill does NOT do:

  • ❌ Does NOT ban natural kratom leaf – the same opioid that sends people to the ER.
  • ❌ Does NOT require pre-market testing for lead, ethanol, salmonella, or other contaminants.
  • ❌ Does NOT fund any enforcement agency – no inspectors, no penalties, no accountability.
  • ❌ Does NOT mandate warning labels about addiction, withdrawal, or drug interactions.
  • ❌ Does NOT close the online sales loophole – kids can still buy with fake IDs.

The bill's "restrictions" on residual solvents (USP <467>) sound meaningful – but without mandatory lab testing or random inspections, they are purely theoretical. No one checks. No one enforces. The industry self‑certifies. That's not regulation. That's a joke.

⚠️ Why "Natural" Kratom Is Still Dangerous

The bill's defenders will say: "We banned synthetic kratom. Natural is fine." That's a lie. Natural kratom contains mitragynine – a partial mu‑opioid agonist with the same addiction profile as classic opioids. It causes physical dependence, withdrawal, and has been detected in hundreds of overdose deaths.

🧪 Lab evidence (see our Evidence page for full reports):
• Lead: Popular kratom powders tested at 0.6–1.0 µg/g lead. At typical daily use (5–10g), that's 3–10 µg of lead per day – a known reproductive and neurodevelopmental toxicant.
• Ethanol: Liquid kratom "shots" have tested at 15‑16% ethanol – legally alcoholic. Kansas ABC seized identical products as unlicensed alcohol.
• No safety standard: No maximum daily dose. No contraindications. No pregnancy warning.

HB 4641 does nothing to stop lead or hidden alcohol. It doesn't even require a label. It leaves the entire contaminated, unregulated market intact – and calls it "consumer protection."

🎯 What Advocates Must Demand (Use These Bullet Points)

When you email or call, focus on these specific failures. Don't get distracted by emotional stories – stick to the facts.

  • ➤ The loophole is intentional. HB 4641 bans only synthetic alkaloids. Natural kratom – the actual product sold – remains legal. That is a gift to the industry.
  • ➤ No contaminant testing. Lead, ethanol, bacteria – none are tested before sale. The bill references USP solvent standards but provides no enforcement. It's a paper tiger.
  • ➤ No enforcement funding. No agency is assigned or funded to inspect products or penalize violators. The bill creates obligations with zero oversight.
  • ➤ No age verification that works. Online sales can bypass age checks. Gas station clerks don't card. The bill doesn't fix this.
  • ➤ Opioids in vape shops are wrong. Even if kratom were perfectly pure, it is still an unregulated opioid agonist. No amount of labeling makes that acceptable.
📢 Sample demand: "Strike the word 'synthetically derived' from Section 2. Ban ALL kratom alkaloids, whether natural or synthetic. Fund mandatory pre‑market testing for heavy metals and ethanol. Assign enforcement to DHEC with real penalties."
