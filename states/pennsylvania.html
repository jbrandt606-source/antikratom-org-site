PHILADELPHIA LEADS KEY ORGANIZATIONS

Philadelphia Banned Retail Kratom – A Model for Pennsylvania

In a landmark public health move, Philadelphia has banned the retail sale of all kratom products within city limits. This action recognizes kratom for what it is: an unregulated opioid with no accepted medical use, documented contamination with lead and ethanol, and clear abuse potential. The ban removes kratom from gas stations, vape shops, and other retail outlets across the city.

What Philadelphia did: A full retail ban on kratom products. No 2% caps, no warning labels, no age restrictions – just a complete removal from the marketplace.

This is a powerful example for other Pennsylvania cities and municipalities. If Philadelphia can ban kratom, so can Pittsburgh, Allentown, Erie, Reading, Scranton, and every other city and borough across the commonwealth. Local action can fill the vacuum left by state inaction.

What you can do right now: Contact your city council, mayor, or borough officials. Ask them to follow Philadelphia's lead and ban retail kratom sales in your community. Use the evidence on this page and our Evidence page to make your case.

Pennsylvania Status – No Statewide Ban, No KCPA Yet

Pennsylvania has not enacted a Kratom Consumer Protection Act or a statewide ban. That means kratom products – powders, capsules, extracts, shots – remain legally available in gas stations, vape shops, and online retailers across most of the state. The kratom industry is actively lobbying for KCPA bills nationwide. These laws create the illusion of regulation while leaving dangerous products on shelves, imposing unenforceable 2% potency caps, and often preempting local bans.

Philadelphia proved a ban is possible and enforceable. The city took action to protect its residents. Now it's time for the rest of Pennsylvania to do the same – both at the local level and through a statewide Schedule I prohibition.

Your job right now: Build a coalition. Document retail sales. Educate lawmakers and local officials. Recruit medical, pharmacy, and law enforcement voices. Tell your legislators to reject any future KCPA and pass a complete ban – and tell your city council to ban retail sales immediately.

What You Can Do Right Now

  • Contact your city council or mayor. Ask them to follow Philadelphia's lead and ban retail kratom sales in your city or borough. Use Philadelphia's success as a template.
  • Document retail sales in your community. Take photos of kratom displays in gas stations, vape shops, and stores near schools. Capture extracts, powders, shots, and any wellness or intoxication marketing. These images build a powerful record for local officials and lawmakers.
  • Build local awareness. Many Pennsylvanians don't know that kratom is an unregulated opioid that contains lead and alcohol – or that Philadelphia has already banned it. Share educational materials with parents, healthcare professionals, educators, local officials, and community groups.
  • Recruit stakeholders. Use the list below to connect with pharmacists, physicians, nurses, addiction professionals, law enforcement, educators, and parents. Ask each organization to support local bans and a statewide full ban.
  • Follow Philadelphia's example. Share news of the Philadelphia ban with your local officials. Show them that a retail ban is practical, enforceable, and already working in Pennsylvania.
  • Prepare for the next legislative session. Legislative action moves quickly. Build local networks, share educational materials, document retail activity, contact community leaders, and prepare testimony and evidence packets for lawmakers to demand a full Schedule I ban – not a weak KCPA.

I urge you to instead support legislation that places all kratom alkaloids under Schedule I – a complete ban. No registration, no 2% cap, no preemption. Only a full ban protects our communities.

Thank you."
