Iowa: SF2192 + HF2133 = Real Bans

Two bills moving through the Iowa legislature would finally prohibit dangerous kratom products. The industry is terrified – they're calling them “blanket bans” because that’s exactly what we need.

SUPPORT BOTH BILLS → CONTACT SENATORS

⚡ WHAT'S HAPPENING IN IOWA

Lawmakers in Des Moines are considering SF 2192 and HF 2133 – two bills that would prohibit the sale and possession of kratom products. The kratom industry is in full panic mode, claiming the bills are “blanket bans on natural kratom leaf.”

🔥 Good. That's the point. Kratom is an unregulated opioid sold in gas stations. It contains lead. It contains hidden alcohol. It causes addiction and has been linked to overdose deaths. A blanket ban is exactly what public health demands.

The industry’s lobbyists are pushing for “targeted safeguards” – which really means keeping kratom on shelves with weak age restrictions and no real testing. Don’t fall for it. Support SF2192 and HF2133. Ban kratom in Iowa.

📜 Why a “Blanket Ban” Is the Only Answer

The kratom industry says they want “responsible regulation.” What they actually want is to keep selling an opioid with no pre‑market safety review, no contaminant testing, and no enforcement. Here’s why a full ban is necessary:

  • It's an opioid. Mitragynine is a mu‑opioid agonist – same mechanism as morphine. It causes physical dependence and withdrawal.
  • It's contaminated. Independent labs have found lead (up to 1 µg/g) and ethanol (15‑16%) in top-selling brands.
  • It's killing people. CDC's SUDORS database shows hundreds of overdose deaths with mitragynine detected.
  • Regulation doesn't work. Age limits are useless when gas station clerks don't card. Labeling doesn't remove lead.
🛑 The industry’s “bad actors” argument is a lie. They claim only “bad actors” sell contaminated or synthetic products. But independent testing found lead and ethanol in major, name‑brand products. The whole industry is the problem.

🎭 Industry Talking Points – Debunked

  • “We support banning synthetic 7‑OH, not natural leaf” – Natural leaf contains mitragynine, which converts to 7‑OH in the body. It's still an opioid. And “natural” doesn't mean safe – hemlock is natural.
  • “Adults should have the right to choose” – Adults don't have the right to buy unregulated opioids in gas stations. That's why heroin and fentanyl are illegal.
  • “Regulation, not prohibition, is the answer” – Regulation hasn't worked. States with KCPA laws still have lead, ethanol, and ER visits. Only prohibition removes the product entirely.
  • “Veterans use kratom for PTSD” – Veterans deserve evidence‑based treatment, not unregulated gas‑station opioids with no safety data. The FDA has not approved kratom for any condition.
