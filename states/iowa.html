⚡ What Happened – And Why It’s Not Over

The Iowa legislature has adjourned sine die without advancing a statewide kratom ban bill. That means SF 2192 and HF 2133 – bills that would have prohibited kratom – did not become law this session.

🔥 But the fight is far from finished. Across the country, lawmakers, pharmacy boards, poison centers, physicians, parents, and law enforcement are confronting the growing public health crisis of kratom – especially high‑potency extracts sold in vape shops, smoke shops, and gas stations.

The next legislative session begins long before lawmakers return to the Capitol. Public policy is shaped in the months between sessions – by the evidence you gather, the allies you recruit, and the voices you organize. That work starts today.

✅ What You Can Do Right Now

  • 📸 Document retail sales in your community. Take photos of vape shop displays, gas station products, extract shots, products sold near schools, “wellness” or “intoxication” marketing, and any advertising that appears to target youth. These images build a powerful record for lawmakers.
  • 📢 Build local awareness. Many Iowans still don’t know that kratom is widely sold in their communities, that potent extracts are openly marketed, that some products have raised contamination concerns (lead, ethanol), and that proposed “regulation” models are often unenforceable. Share educational materials with parents, healthcare professionals, educators, local officials, and community groups.
  • 🤝 Help identify Iowa stakeholders. We are working to connect with pharmacists, physicians, nurses, addiction professionals, law enforcement, educators, and parents concerned about retail kratom sales. If you are interested in helping during the next legislative session, contact us.
  • 👀 Follow legislative & regulatory developments. Other states continue to debate statewide bans, synthetic‑only restrictions, Kratom Consumer Protection Acts (KCPAs), and pharmacy board scheduling actions. These developments frequently influence future Iowa legislation.
  • 📋 Prepare for the next session. Legislative action moves quickly once session begins. Help now by building local networks, sharing educational materials, documenting retail activity, contacting community leaders, and preparing testimony and evidence packets for lawmakers.
