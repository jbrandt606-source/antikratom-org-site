📜 HB 2415: A Bill That Protects the Industry, Not Arizonans

HB 2415 pretends to regulate kratom, but it is filled with fatal loopholes that leave dangerous products on gas station shelves. Here’s what the bill actually does:

  • ❌ Only bans “synthetic” kratom – natural leaf and natural extracts remain 100% legal.
  • ❌ Creates a meaningless potency loophole – the bill only bans 7‑hydroxymitragynine if it exceeds 800 parts per million (ppm). But manufacturers can simply make bigger tablets or capsules to keep the total dose high while the concentration stays under 800 ppm. A 500mg pill at 700 ppm delivers the same amount of drug as a 250mg pill at 1400 ppm. The threshold is pure theater.
  • ❌ Law enforcement is NOT required to test – the bill explicitly says agencies don’t have to test if they lack the capability. So in practice, no one will enforce anything.
  • ❌ No field test exists – officers cannot distinguish “legal” natural kratom from “illegal” synthetic or high‑concentration products at the point of sale.
  • ❌ Kratom stays in gas stations and vape shops – the bill only adds advertising restrictions and age limits, which are routinely ignored.
🚨 The “800 ppm” loophole is a gift to manufacturers. A company can sell a potent extract pill that delivers a massive dose of 7‑OH while staying under the limit. This bill does not protect anyone – it gives the industry a playbook.

🔬 The Bill Admits It Cannot Be Enforced

Section 17 of HB 2415 states: “Law enforcement agencies and crime laboratories are not required to test or analyze products to identify the presence of kratom, or to distinguish between natural and synthetic kratom, if the law enforcement agency or laboratory does not possess the necessary capability.”

⚠️ Read that again. The bill literally says police don’t have to enforce the law if they can’t test for it. Since no Arizona agency currently has the capability to distinguish natural from synthetic kratom, this bill creates zero enforcement. It is a paper tiger.

The Attorney General can coordinate testing through a qualified lab – but that is expensive, slow, and will never happen for routine retail violations. In practice, HB 2415 changes nothing. Kratom will continue to be sold as before.

✅ The Only Fix: Amend HB 2415 to a Full Schedule I Ban

We need a clean amendment that strikes the synthetic‑only and 800 ppm language and instead places all kratom alkaloids – natural, synthetic, extracts, whole leaf – under Schedule I of Arizona’s controlled substances list.

🎯 Your exact ask to every legislator – copy this:
“Amend HB 2415 to remove the synthetic‑only and 800 ppm exceptions. Place ALL kratom alkaloids under Schedule I. Without this change, the bill is unenforceable and leaves deadly products on the market.”

A full Schedule I ban is the only approach that:

  • ✅ Removes all kratom products from gas stations, vape shops, and online retailers.
  • ✅ Eliminates the need for impossible synthetic‑natural distinctions.
  • ✅ Aligns Arizona with the FDA’s position and other states’ Board of Pharmacy determinations.
  • ✅ Stops the contamination crisis – lead, ethanol, adulterants – by removing the product entirely.
