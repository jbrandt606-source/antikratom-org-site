📜 HB 328: A Real Ban on Kratom

HB 328 would add natural kratom leaf to North Carolina's Schedule VI controlled substances list. That means complete prohibition – no sales, no possession, no loopholes. For the first time, North Carolina would treat kratom like other dangerous drugs with no accepted medical use.

✅ Why a ban is necessary: Kratom is a partial mu‑opioid agonist – the same mechanism as morphine and oxycodone. It causes physical dependence, withdrawal, and has been linked to overdose deaths. Schedule VI is exactly where it belongs.

The kratom industry is lobbying fiercely to kill this bill. They want to keep selling an unregulated, contaminated product to anyone with cash. Don't let them.

⚠️ Why Kratom Must Be Banned

  • It's an opioid. Mitragynine acts on mu‑opioid receptors – proven addiction liability, withdrawal syndrome, and overdose risk.
  • It's contaminated. Independent lab tests show lead at 0.6–1.0 µg/g and ethanol as high as 15‑16% in popular extracts.
  • It's killing people. The CDC's SUDORS database documents hundreds of deaths with mitragynine detected.
  • It has no medical use. The FDA has rejected every attempt to recognize kratom as a dietary supplement or medicine.
  • Regulation doesn't work. Age limits and labels don't remove lead. Only a ban removes the product entirely.
🛑 The industry's “natural” lie: Arsenic is natural. Lead is natural. “Natural” does not mean safe. Kratom is an opioid – and opioids have no place in gas stations.
