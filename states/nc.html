Why Kratom Must Be Scheduled – The Evidence

  • It is an opioid. Mitragynine is a partial mu-opioid agonist – same mechanism as morphine, oxycodone, and heroin.
  • It causes addiction and withdrawal. Thousands report severe withdrawal: anxiety, insomnia, muscle pain, diarrhea.
  • It is contaminated. Independent labs have found lead (up to 1 µg/g) and ethanol (15-16%) in top-selling brands.
  • It is killing people. CDC's SUDORS database shows hundreds of overdose deaths with mitragynine detected.
  • No medical use. The FDA has rejected every attempt to approve kratom for any condition.
Schedule IV is the right tool. It allows for criminal penalties for sale and possession, sends a clear public health message, and gives law enforcement the ability to remove kratom from stores.
