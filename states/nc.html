⚡ The Bill Died – But We Can Still Win

A bill that would have banned natural kratom leaf in North Carolina died in the House. The kratom industry lobbied hard to kill it. But that doesn't mean we give up.

🔥 New strategy: We are now asking House members to add kratom as a Schedule IV controlled substance – either through an amendment to a pending bill or by introducing new standalone legislation. Schedule IV is the appropriate classification for drugs with abuse potential and no accepted medical use.

The industry wants you to think the fight is over. It's not. Your representative needs to hear from you now. Tell them: Schedule kratom as IV. No more gas‑station opioids.

📜 Why Kratom Must Be Scheduled

  • It's an opioid. Mitragynine is a partial mu‑opioid agonist – same mechanism as morphine, oxycodone, and heroin.
  • It causes addiction and withdrawal. Thousands report severe withdrawal: anxiety, insomnia, muscle pain, diarrhea.
  • It's contaminated. Independent labs have found lead (up to 1 µg/g) and ethanol (15‑16%) in top-selling brands.
  • It's killing people. CDC's SUDORS database shows hundreds of overdose deaths with mitragynine detected.
  • No medical use. The FDA has rejected every attempt to approve kratom for any condition.
📢 Schedule IV is the right tool. It allows for criminal penalties for sale and possession, sends a clear public health message, and gives law enforcement the ability to remove kratom from stores.

🎯 What to Tell Your House Member

  • If a relevant bill is moving, offer an amendment to add kratom to Schedule IV.
  • If no vehicle exists, introduce a new standalone bill.
  • Reject industry talking points. Regulation doesn't work – only scheduling removes the product entirely.
