📜 SB 557: A Synthetic‑Only Ban That Cannot Be Enforced

SB 557, as currently written, prohibits only “synthetic or semi‑synthetic” kratom products. But there is no field test, no point‑of‑sale verification, and no reliable way for law enforcement or retailers to distinguish synthetic from natural. All products look the same, are sold in the same gas stations and vape shops, and are labeled identically.

🚨 The fatal flaw: A bill that cannot be enforced is not a ban – it’s a loophole large enough to drive the entire industry through. The current language leaves “natural” kratom completely untouched.

Meanwhile, a state Board of Pharmacy elsewhere has already conducted a full 8‑factor review and unanimously determined that all kratom alkaloids meet Schedule I criteria – not just synthetics. New Hampshire should not create a weaker, contradictory framework.

⚡ The Only Way This Changes: Committee Amendment

Once SB 557 leaves the House Finance Committee, your leverage drops fast. Right now is the last meaningful window. We need a House member – preferably on the Finance Committee – to introduce an amendment that:

  • ✅ Removes the “synthetic‑only” language
  • ✅ Expands to all kratom alkaloids (mitragynine, 7‑hydroxymitragynine, and any derivative)
  • ✅ Places them under Schedule I – a full ban
🎯 Your exact ask:
“Amend SB 557 to include all kratom alkaloids, including mitragynine and 7‑hydroxymitragynine, under Schedule I rather than limiting the bill to synthetic or semi‑synthetic compounds.”

🔥 Your Core Argument (What Moves Legislators)

  • Enforcement failure: No field test. No way to distinguish synthetic vs natural at point of sale. The bill is unenforceable as written.
  • Regulatory contradiction: A state Board of Pharmacy has already unanimously concluded kratom meets Schedule I criteria across the board – not just synthetics. NH would be ignoring expert review.
  • Retail reality: Same stores, same products, same labeling. “Synthetic vs natural” is meaningless in commerce. The bill leaves a massive loophole.
