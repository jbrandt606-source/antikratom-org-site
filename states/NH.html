New Hampshire: Prepare for a Full Ban Next Session

SB 557 died, but the fight continues. The next kratom bill must be a full Schedule I ban — no synthetic loopholes, no unenforceable language. Build the groundwork now.

The Lesson from SB 557: Synthetic-Only Won't Work

SB 557 failed — in part because it was built on a fatal flaw: it banned only “synthetic or semi‑synthetic” kratom products. Lawmakers realized that distinction is unenforceable. No field test exists. Retailers can't tell the difference. It left a giant loophole for “natural” kratom.

We must not repeat this mistake. The next bill must be a clean, enforceable full ban on all kratom alkaloids — mitragynine, 7‑hydroxymitragynine, and all derivatives — placed directly under Schedule I.

A state Board of Pharmacy has already conducted a full review and unanimously determined that kratom alkaloids meet Schedule I criteria. New Hampshire deserves a bill that matches that expert conclusion — not a watered-down version that invites failure.

Our Goal for Next Year's Session

With SB 557 gone, we have a fresh start. But we can't afford to wait until the last minute. We need to build relationships and educate legislators right now so that when the next session begins, a strong, full-ban bill is ready to go.

  • Secure a House member — preferably on the Finance or Criminal Justice Committee — to sponsor a new bill that bans all kratom alkaloids.
  • Ensure the bill language is clean and enforceable, avoiding the synthetic distinction altogether.
  • Build a coalition of state agencies, medical groups, and law enforcement to testify in support.
Your role right now: Start the conversation. Contact your representatives, share the evidence, and tell them: "Next session, I expect you to support a full kratom ban — no loopholes."

Your Core Argument (What Moves Legislators)

  • Enforceability matters: A synthetic-only ban cannot be enforced — no field test, no way to distinguish at point of sale. A full ban on the alkaloids themselves is the only practical path.
  • Expert consensus: Board of Pharmacy reviews have already concluded kratom alkaloids meet Schedule I criteria. New Hampshire should align with that expert judgment.
  • Public health reality: Kratom is linked to lead poisoning, ethanol contamination, and rising overdose deaths. Half-measures put New Hampshire families at risk.

Preparing Written Testimony for Next Session

Even though there's no bill yet, you can submit written materials to committee members to educate them in advance. Keep it to one page. Structure:

  1. Your name, city, and that you are a New Hampshire resident.
  2. State your expectation for next session: a full ban on all kratom alkaloids.
  3. Explain why the synthetic-only language fails — no field test, no retail verification.
  4. Cite the Board of Pharmacy determination (unanimous Schedule I for all alkaloids).
  5. End with a clear ask: "Please sponsor or support a bill that places all kratom alkaloids under Schedule I."

Attach evidence from our Evidence page (lead reports, ethanol findings, CDC death data).
