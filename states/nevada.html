Subject: Official Comment – LCB File No. R038-26 – Schedule I Classification of Kratom Alkaloids

To the Nevada State Board of Pharmacy,

I am submitting this comment for inclusion in the official record regarding LCB File No. R038-26.

The proposal to place kratom alkaloids into Schedule I raises a fundamental issue of regulatory consistency and evidentiary integrity.

The substances at issue—mitragynine and 7‑hydroxymitragynine—are being considered for the most restrictive classification under Nevada law. That classification requires a clear showing of high abuse potential, no accepted medical use, and a lack of safety even under medical supervision. The current record does not cleanly support that conclusion when applied broadly to all kratom‑derived material.

The data being cited in support of scheduling does not distinguish in a meaningful or enforceable way between:
• raw botanical material
• concentrated extracts
• products adulterated with non‑kratom substances

This is not a minor technical gap—it is a fatal enforcement problem. There is no practical field test available to regulators, law enforcement, or retailers that can reliably differentiate mitragynine from 7‑hydroxymitragynine concentrations in finished products or distinguish naturally occurring alkaloids from synthetically altered forms.

A Schedule I classification without a corresponding, enforceable testing framework does not create control—it creates ambiguity and selective enforcement.

Additionally, publicly available certificates of analysis for commercially sold kratom products demonstrate variability not only in alkaloid content, but also in contaminants, including heavy metals. This introduces a separate public health issue that is not addressed by blanket scheduling and instead highlights the limitations of the current regulatory approach.

If the Board proceeds, it should clearly articulate:
1. The specific evidentiary basis for applying Schedule I criteria to all kratom alkaloids collectively
2. The analytical methods that will be used to enforce this classification in real‑world settings
3. How this action avoids relying on data derived from materially different product categories

Absent that clarity, this action risks being both overbroad and operationally unworkable.

This comment is submitted for the official record in advance of further action on R038-26.

Respectfully,
[Your Name]
[City, State]
