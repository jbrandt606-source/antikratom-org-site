CONTACT STATE MEDICAL SOCIETIES

Doctors' voices carry weight that lobbyists can't buy. When state medical societies speak, legislators listen. Make sure they're speaking the truth about kratom.

Why Medical Societies? Find Your Society

Why State Medical Societies Hold Power

State medical societies represent thousands of physicians – the people on the front lines of the kratom crisis. They issue position statements, educate their members, testify before legislatures, and shape public opinion. When a medical society calls kratom a public health threat, it changes the debate.

The industry's fear: that doctors will see through their "natural supplement" marketing and recognize kratom for what it is – an unapproved, adulterated substance causing addiction, withdrawal, and death.

⛔ Why Your Voice Matters

Medical societies are membership organizations. They respond to their members – and to concerned citizens like you. A flood of emails from parents, community members, and patients forces them to act.

What to Demand From Your State Medical Society

  • Issue a public health warning. Ask them to publish a statement warning physicians and the public about kratom's risks: addiction, withdrawal, liver toxicity, and interaction with other drugs.
  • Adopt a formal position supporting a ban. Urge them to go on record supporting legislation that removes kratom from retail stores.
  • Educate their members. Request that they distribute information to physicians about recognizing kratom use, managing withdrawal, and reporting adverse events.
  • Testify at legislative hearings. Ask them to send a representative to speak in favor of bans or against industry-written "KCPA" bills.
  • Oppose industry-backed KCPAs. Make sure they understand that KCPAs do not make kratom safe – they just create a false sense of security.
  • Publish an article in their journal or newsletter. Many state medical societies have publications. Ask them to feature a story on kratom's dangers.

Sample Message to Your State Medical Society

Adapt this with your personal story and local context.

To: [State Medical Society contact email] Subject: URGENT: Please take a stand against unregulated kratom sales Dear [Executive Director / Public Policy Director], My name is [Your Name], and I am a resident of [City, State]. I am writing to urge the [State] Medical Society to take a strong public position against the unregulated sale of kratom. Kratom (mitragynine) is a psychoactive substance with opioid-like effects, sold openly in gas stations and vape shops. The FDA has declared kratom products adulterated and has warned of liver failure, seizures, addiction, and death. Poison control centers report thousands of exposures each year, including hundreds of children. In [State], we have seen [mention any local cases or data]. Despite this, the kratom industry is pushing so-called "Kratom Consumer Protection Acts" that create the illusion of regulation while leaving the underlying dangers untouched. These bills are written by the industry itself and do nothing to warn consumers about opioid-like withdrawal or prevent addiction. Physicians in [State] are on the front lines – treating patients in withdrawal, managing seizures, and reporting adverse events. Yet many doctors are unaware of kratom's prevalence or its pharmacology. Your society has the power to change that. I ask the [State] Medical Society to: - Issue a public warning about kratom's risks. - Adopt a formal position supporting legislation to ban retail sales. - Educate members through newsletters, CME, or alerts. - Oppose any KCPA that pretends to regulate this danger. - Testify at hearings in favor of real public health measures. The industry counts on silence. Please don't let them have it. Thank you for protecting the health of [State]'s families. [Your Name] [Your Phone Number] [Your Email]

⛔ Tips for Maximum Impact

  • Personalize it. If you're a physician, say so. If you're a patient or family member, share your story. Personal connection cuts through.
  • Reference your state's data. Use poison center numbers, local news reports, or hospital admissions if available.
  • Ask for a response. Request that they inform you of any action taken. Follow up if you don't hear back.
  • Connect with other advocates. If multiple people write, it's harder to ignore. Share this page with friends.
  • Attend meetings. Many medical societies have open board meetings or annual conferences. Show up and speak.

Legislators trust their own physicians. When a state medical society speaks, it's not just another interest group – it's the collective voice of the people who save lives every day. Make sure that voice is on our side.
