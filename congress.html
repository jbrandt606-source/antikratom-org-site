Why Your Voice Is Urgently Needed

The kratom industry spends millions on lobbyists and high‑profile campaigns. They tell Congress that kratom is “natural” and “safe” – while their own legal disclaimers admit it acts on opioid receptors and can cause dependence and withdrawal.

  • FDA: Rejected kratom as a dietary ingredient, stating it could not establish basic safety.

  • Poison control: 8,555 exposures (2019‑2024), including 700+ children.
  • Industry “regulation”: KCPA bills hide addiction risks and leave children at risk.

Lawmakers hear from industry representatives every day. They need to hear from the people they serve. Your letter can tip the balance.

📄 Include a Flyer (Keep It One Page)

A short, evidence‑backed flyer can make your message even more powerful. Use one from our Media & Flyers Toolkit – but remember to keep it to one page. Legislators and their staff are overwhelmed. A single, sharp page of key facts is more likely to be read and kept.

  • Pick the most relevant flyer: FDA warnings, CDC death data, pediatric poisonings, or the legal failures of KCPAs.
  • Attach it to your email with a brief note: “Attached is a one‑page summary of federal data.”
  • If you’re calling, offer to send it afterward. “I have a one‑page summary of FDA and CDC data – I’d be happy to email it to your staff.”

🎯 How to Make Your Letter Count

  • Personalize it. Share your story or local details. A letter that mentions a specific store or a local poison control call is far more memorable.
  • Be specific. Ask them to support scheduling kratom or to cosponsor a ban. Vague asks get vague responses.
  • Mention your town. “I live in [City] and shop at the gas station on Main Street where kratom is sold.”
  • Keep it under one page. If you attach a flyer, it should also be one page. Respect their time.
  • Follow up. If you don’t hear back in two weeks, call their office. A follow‑up call can move a letter from the “read” pile to the “act” pile.
