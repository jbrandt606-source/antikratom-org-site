📋 Documented evidence of harm

Peer‑reviewed studies show kratom linked to seizures, liver toxicity, and severe withdrawal. The industry dismisses adverse event reports, but internal documents reveal they track them closely.

Key finding: More than 50% of kratom products tested contain heavy metals or salmonella.

1,170
adverse events reported to FDA since 2010

☎️ Poison control centers ring the alarm

According to America's Poison Centers, kratom exposures jumped from 26 calls in 2010 to over 2,100 in 2023. Most involve serious medical outcomes — especially when mixed with alcohol or other drugs.

2,137
exposure calls in 2023 alone

Moderate to severe outcomes reported in 42% of cases (poison control data).

⚰️ CDC: deaths involving kratom

CDC analysis found kratom was a contributing cause in at least 187 deaths between 2016–2022. In most cases, other substances were present — but kratom was the common factor. The industry often blames “contaminated” products, yet pure kratom alone caused fatal toxicity.

91
deaths where kratom was the only substance (CDC)

The true number is likely higher due to underreporting.

🏛️ FDA: no safe use, no approved indications

The FDA has repeatedly warned that kratom is an opioid and carries similar risks of abuse, addiction, and death. They seized tons of kratom products and published analyses showing dangerous levels of lead and nickel.

7
FDA import alerts & mandatory detention

“There is no reliable evidence to support the use of kratom as a treatment for any medical condition.” – FDA statement
